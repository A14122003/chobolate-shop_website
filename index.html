About

Chocolate is a popular treat made from the seeds of the cacao tree, known scientifically as Theobroma cacao. Native to Central and South America, cacao has been used for thousands of years, originally by ancient civilizations like the Maya and Aztecs, who consumed it as a bitter, spiced drink. Modern chocolate is created through a complex process that involves fermenting, drying, roasting, and grinding cacao beans to produce cocoa mass, which can then be separated into cocoa solids and cocoa butter.
